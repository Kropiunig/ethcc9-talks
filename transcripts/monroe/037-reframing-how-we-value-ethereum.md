# Reframing How we Value Ethereum
**William** (Ethereum Market Research Center) --- Monroe Stage

Hello. Last talk of the day. So I'm going to reframe how we value Ethereum, not just as a blockchain competing against any other blockchain on some metric, but as a public goods infrastructure unlike any other blockchain out there. To do that, I'm going to present to you a valuation framework for applying this valuation, because if we apply the wrong framework, we're going to misprice Ethereum. As you know very well today, Ethereum is mispriced, right? We don't want it to be at the price it is today. I wrote this report last December. And my talk is almost like an executive summary version of it. What drove me to this conclusion and this work is the fact that I kept asking myself why aren't fundamentals driving valuations in the crypto space? As you well know, just about anything that is not related to crypto moves crypto, right? One day, it's the interest rates, it's the inflation, now it's the war. Sometime it's another factor like what the technology world is doing. It's always a risk-on asset. It's the first asset class that's taking a hit if there is any heat in the markets, and it's the last one to recover. So I kept asking myself, why can't crypto decide of their future themselves? And to do that, I started to understand what the different segments were that we can apply some valuation models. And I concluded there were maybe four segments we can look at, DeFi, revenue chains, bitcoins, and Ethereum. And when we looked at the DeFi protocols, they are probably the most advanced in terms of trying to match some valuation metrics to a price of some sort.
