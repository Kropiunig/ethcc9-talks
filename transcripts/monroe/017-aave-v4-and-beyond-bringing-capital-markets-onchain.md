So, what are the main reasons why we went for this kind of design? What we noticed while developing the different iterations of Aave, we noticed that the liquidity provisioning part was pretty much common across all the iterations of the protocol that we had. And the only thing that was evolving and changing over time was the borrowing approach, right? So we were keeping adding features on the borrowing side while the liquidity provisioning was staying more or less stable over the different iterations. So the idea came to actually abstract away the liquidity provisioning part from the borrowing. So this opens up a lot in terms of design space because now we can have pluggable borrowing strategies that can actually be integrated into one single hub, all of them sharing the same liquidity. So this allows us to actually move away from one individual borrowing strategy, which is deeply tied in how the protocol works, and actually extend the protocol functional capabilities by adding different borrowing strategies that might be following very heterogeneous approaches, okay? We also added the first iteration of the protocol, which is the one that we deployed yesterday, covers pretty much the same use case as Aave V3 does in a more efficient way because besides having added new features, which we'll be reviewing quickly here, we also improved a variety of aspects in terms of efficiency, gas costs, and so on. Also comes with a variety of new features. I'm gonna just list a few. There are many, but the most important ones. And I'm also going to point out to the docs at the end of the presentation. We have, I would say the most important ones are the risk premiums and the dynamic risk configuration. So risk premiums are a way for the protocol to price collateral risk independently. This was not possible with Aave V3 because all the assets that were listed as collateral in Aave V3 were sharing the same borrowing costs. With V4, we introduced a new risk lever for risk managers to be able to price risk more appropriately or to manage assets whose risk parameters and risk profile change more drastically over time. Another, the other feature is dynamic risk configuration where we have now the possibility of changing the configurations for the collaterals in a way that doesn't affect all the borrowers like it was happening before with previous iterations, but we can basically change the risk configuration only for new users, which opens up to the possibility of having more granular risk control and a better approach towards managing changing market conditions. We also have other features that are very, very important. We added liquidations in a Dutch auction format, which means that now if the risk managers believe it appropriate, the liquidations can be more optimized towards reducing exposure to the protocol or giving more possibilities to borrowers in terms of more optimized liquidations. We have a reinvestment module feature, which in my opinion, especially to this day where the market is a bit on a depressed condition, is very, very important because this will allow the DAO to actually move liquidity across lower risk strategies to optimize yield in within other markets.
