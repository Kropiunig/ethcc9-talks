Okay, so just a quick primer for those less familiar with ZK, or zero-knowledge. Zero-knowledge proof gear lets you prove a computation was correct without revealing the underlying data. ZK gives you mathematical certainty with no trusted party and no data disclosure at all. Why do institutions care? Because you can prove compliance without exposing positions. You can verify a counterparty executed correctly without seeing their book. You can coordinate across ledgers without sharing state. And regulators can verify correctness without ever touching the raw data. So that's the primitive that makes this whole architecture possible. How it works. So on the left, you have private execution. So transactions run inside a permissioned Hyperledger Besu cluster with role-based access control and policy enforcement. Only authorized participants can see the data. The sequencer, or block builder, enforces compliance before a block is even produced. In the middle, you have ZK proving pipeline. So a prover takes the execution trace and generates a cryptographic proof that everything was executed or computed correctly without revealing any of the underlying data. Now you have 128-bit quantum-safe security. And that's not on the roadmap. That's there today, live. And on the right-hand side, you have a proof that settles on Ethereum L1.
