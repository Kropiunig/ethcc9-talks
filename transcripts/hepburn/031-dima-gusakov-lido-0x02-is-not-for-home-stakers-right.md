First things first, what's OX02? And I had a pretty good joke here about OX02 being a decimal digit in a hexadecimal form, but let's skip it for now. And go straight to two types of withdrawal credentials for Ethereum validators. Meet Alice and Bob. Alice is running a, let's call it traditional OX01 validator, and she deposits 32 ETH to her validator. Bob deposits the same 32 ETH, but to OX02 validator. And at this point, there is absolutely no difference between the experience that Alice and Bob have. But as soon as Alice keeps running her validator, and a bit of a rewards starts accumulating on her balance, because of the fact that for OX01 validator, two crucial values, being min activation balance and max effective balance, are essentially the same and equal to 32 ETH. It means that anything accumulated above 32 ETH for Alice, or for her OX01 validator, would be swept to her execution layer withdrawal credentials. And with the current sweep cycle being roughly nine days, I believe, each every nine days, Alice receives a bit of a rewards. So what happens to Bob? Well, Bob, since he is running OX02 validator, which is also called compounding validator, he will just accumulate his rewards on his validator balance, and nothing will be swept. Well, not for, like, infinite period of time. There is a threshold, however, to reach this threshold, we need to zoom out a little bit. Like that. And then, it will take us an absolute eternity to wait for the rewards to accumulate to this threshold, so let's imagine that Bob also made a few additional deposits to his validator. And now Bob is sitting at a whopping 2,048 ETH. Wow, 64 times more than OX1 validator. And after this point, the experience for the Bob would be exactly the same as for Alice, because 2,048 ETH, this is a max effective balance, the max effective balance for OX02 validators. So what we've learned about OX02? OX02 can accumulate or compound balance, while OX1 cannot. OX02 require almost the same hardware resources per validator, other than the requirements for peer-to-peer, which is data availability sampling for blobs, which depends on ETH staked and not on the number of validators. But the rest, like producing attestations, block, and so on, is essentially the same, it doesn't matter which type of credentials you're running. And OX02s are better for the network, because as you've noted, you can fit 64 OX1 validators into one OX02 validator. Which means that if all of the validators that are currently on Ethereum network would magically become OX02 validators, we would see a drastic decrease in the number of validators, hence we will see less P2P traffic, less network congestion, and so on and so forth. So the overall conclusion here is, OX02 validators are good for Ethereum network, good in general, and simply good. But if they are good, why do we see this? And I'm happy to say that yes, there is a way. And actually there are two or maybe even three ways, but let's break it into pieces one by one. And we start with EIP-8148, custom sweep threshold for validators. This EIP is not implemented yet. It had just been proposed. So the reason I'm here telling you about it is to convince you that this is indeed a good solution and we need to have it included in the H-star hard fork. So remember Bob, who had to accumulate 2048 ETH before he start getting his rewards automatically. But the question is, where did this number even came from? And why Bob should be limited to this precise number of 2048 ETH? Well, with EIP-8146, we propose to make this value variable or to be correct, configurable so that Bob would not have to be locked into choice between like, do I want to have 32 ETH or do I want to have 2048 ETH? No, Bob will be able to choose to set a so-called custom threshold and ETH value up until which his OX02 validator balance will accumulate. And after which all of the rewards will be sent to execution layer withdrawal credentials. So with EIP-8148, Bob can, again, as I said, set a desired amount of ETH to accumulate, receive all of the rewards above this amount on execution layer withdrawal credentials in the same way he already does for his OX01 credentials. And he can change the threshold anytime, like anytime and any number of times. And EIP-8148 is also very secure because Bob cannot set a new threshold lower than the current balance so that he cannot bypass withdrawal queues and the rules of the withdrawal queues with this EIP.
