# The On-Chain Euro
**Ernesto Olmedo Pereira** --- Kelly Stage

Good morning everyone. Thank you very much for coming to this short talk on the Keyvalues project. I'm very happy to see a few friendly faces but also a lot of new ones. Today I want to let you know a little bit about what we are thinking. My name is Ernesto. I am originally from El Salvador but I've been working in Germany since 10 years. Recently switched from a bank to Keyvalues. I am head of strategy and DeFi. My background is actually also on DeFi so I don't really consider myself a banker. And what we are trying to do at Keyvalues is basically to issue an open design euro stablecoin backed by 12 banks. Why? Because when you look at what's going on in DeFi today, you see that the euro is basically invisible, right? So you could say, sure, Circle has like 800 million TVL on their euro C stablecoin but if you compare it to the US dollar stablecoins, it's basically nothing, right? So we think we as Europe should have a presence in the fastest growing economy in the world which is the digital economy. The banks in our consortium all agree that the world is going to move on-chain. We think that in the future, you know, non-DeFi people, you know, normal people, will also start using on-chain services but maybe they will not know about it because it's going to be underpinning, you know, the banking apps, the savings apps and so on. So if you believe that thesis, then obviously this is a huge problem if the euro has basically zero presence because this makes us very, you know, dependent from American power. We have nothing against the US, of course, but we want to see a strong Europe which means we also need to have a strong presence on DeFi. Why do we think that DeFi should even care about us? I mean, we are quite humble and we know that, you know, we are banks so obviously there has to be a gap to be closed between us and the ecosystem. When we talk at DeFi protocols and we have very open discussions with them, we mention always this concentration problem that you have basically too much USD presence, which means when Donald Trump has a bad day and decides to start a war somewhere, right, so this becomes an issue for the risk parameters of, for example, lending protocols or in general, you know, DeFi protocols. So we think it's in the best interest of the DeFi community as well to have an option other than the US dollar to build up their economic models. So far we have heard good responses to this idea and we hope that in the future even more DeFi protocols see the need or, you know, get access to this euro alternative to the dollar. The other thing that we are seeing is the regulatory tailwind. So you probably have heard in the past that the central bank in Europe has been a little bit against stablecoins. We are having very honest discussions with them about this because we don't 100% agree that, you know, there has to be either, you know, a centralized option or stablecoins. We think both can live together. Hopefully they interact with each other, but of course there has to be a lot of work for that to happen. But the good news is that the regulators, the banks, the central banks, they all are, you know, staffed by very smart people and they are open to these discussions. They don't have any prejudice. So this gives us a little bit of certainty that, you know, this is not going to become a problem in the future and that we can work together. And we think this gap is an opportunity for builders. So if you think about what does it mean that we want to build an ecosystem, then obviously there is a big opportunity for people to create, you know, new protocols on top of this. So who is Kivalis? We are not a bank. We are a consortium. So we are a completely independent company. We are set up as an e-money institute in Amsterdam, which is owned today by 12 banks. But as I said, this is an open design, so more banks are in dialogue to join us. We are happy that we have all major European banks already, sorry, European countries, represented as part of our shareholder circle. And we are now expanding to, you know, even more countries. So hopefully by the time we go live, we will have basically the entire European Union as part of our consortium, which will be quite amazing if you think about distribution and so on. I don't think there are so many stablecoins out there who can say they have, you know, each bank in Europe as part of the consortium. So we plan to go live by the time we get our EMT license, which hopefully is coming on the second half of this year, depending, you know, on how the process goes. And the value proposition that we are bringing or why are banks even, you know, joining this is because we think in order for the euro to actually have a chance to be used in DeFi or in general in the digital ecosystem, it needs really deep liquidity, which is why all of these banks are supporting us with this. We are going to launch, you know, with all major payers. Obviously, ETH is one of them. And we think that by having this very deep liquidity, we can ensure that builders, you know, can deploy their own ideas on top of this. What does this mean? This means our token is, it has compliance functions, of course. It's an ERC20F. So in principle, anyone can use it, but obviously we have some compliance functions on top of it. So it's as open as it can be while being compliant. And this is where we think the demand is going to be in the beginning. So we are working with the biggest decentralized exchanges and also centralized exchanges in the world. We are going to establish deep liquidity against the US dollar and also against ETH, wrapped Bitcoin, obviously as well. And depending on how we see the ecosystem developing, we will also introduce even more pairs. We are going to have ultra low slippage pools, special pools on the major AMMs. We are talking about, you know, having facilities for RFQs or other prop AMM technologies so that right from the start, you could swap big amounts of, you know, USDC or whatever towards the euro. We are working with major lending protocols as well because we see demand there for institutional grade liquidity. So the US dollar to euro carry trade is a very interesting proposition for many people. And we are also optimizing lending vaults, you know, for isolated markets. So this means, for example, institutional tokenized assets can be used as collateral. And we think at least some banks in our consortium are going to start experimenting with this because it's a very interesting idea to, you know, have a way for banks to kind of have an on-chain repo facility, which is kind of non-existent today. We think DeFi is the engine for what we try to achieve. We don't think this is, you know, a side project. So DeFi is very important for us. First, we want to have deep liquidity on global exchanges, as I mentioned, because we think that's going to be a big distribution channel. We are present in Asia. We are going to launch also in Latin America, in Africa, of course, because we also think it makes no sense for us to try to replace SEPA instant or other, you know, payment protocols in Europe. We think the euro demand is global, so that's why we are going a bit more aggressive into those markets. We are going to see liquidity at decentralized exchanges via market makers. As I said, also some special pools for even deeper liquidity for, you know, institutional grade transactions. We are going to activate on different lending protocols as much as we can, of course. We think there will be a time where you probably have small markets or limited collateral possibilities, but this is going to expand with it in the future. We are also talking to some yield protocols in terms of using collateral, tokenized collateral from our banks or perhaps tokenized credit or some other interesting products to provide yield, so native euro yield. This is going to be open to anyone, so I think it's quite an interesting thing for people who think the euro might be more stable than other currencies, especially with what's going on in the world. And then we intend to bring institutional volume. So as I mentioned, we think carry trades, euro leverage facilities, and fresh euro diversification for corporates is going to be a big thing. But for that to happen first, we need to have deep global liquidity. And we think once we have these deeper integrations, then, you know, the banks and the asset managers of the world will see that the euro has a proven track record, is useful, and we hope that they will start using our stablecoin as well for their own treasury operations. So DeFi is not an afterthought. It's kind of the way for us to build an ecosystem for the euro. And we actually also think in terms of institutional DeFi, so how can we integrate our banks,


Our asset managers into the ecosystem out there. We are not planning to, you know, launch our own protocols. We think it's much better to work with what's out there and try to bridge the gap between TradFi and DeFi. So we see three possibilities for TradFi to, you know, leverage DeFi. One of them is, of course, the euro-US dollar carry trade, leverage strategies for the euro, treasury diversification, and hopefully there will be even more ideas out there in the future. These are the three ones that we are pushing right now. And this is more or less the roadmap that we have. Once we achieve institutional familiarity, which is what I was describing previously, we see a few institutional use cases. So one of them is cross-border payments. We have quite a few global banks in our consortium, and we think it will be quite interesting for them to move money between, you know, Asia, Europe, Africa, Latin America, using a stablecoin instead of the usual rails. They see this as quite a good opportunity to become even more efficient. This is going to happen, perhaps, well, I mean, we're going to launch on Ethereum mainnet, and we are talking to the Ethereum Foundation for, you know, solutions for privacy and also other institutional requirements that you may have for this. Then we have security settlement. There are some very interesting ideas here in Europe about, for example, having a digitally native sovereign debt, which will be super cool to have, right, because then you can have a native reserve for the stablecoin. You could have, for example, a sovereign bond that is natively issued on Ethereum or some other chain and have this as part of the treasury, which means then you have even more flexibility for redemptions, which is a bit of a challenge today with how stablecoins operate. And then finally, we see trade finance. Lots of corporates are asking us, okay, so how do I use this stablecoin for, you know, all of my operations globally? And for this, it also helps to have the banks because they can be the on and off ramps in a global scale, which we think it's also quite a good USP to have as a newcomer stablecoin, right? So we don't have to worry too much about this. And here's where we are today. So we took about two years to prepare this launch because you can imagine how hard it is to sell this idea to banks. So it's been a lot of work onboarding them. We started with nine banks. As I said, we are now 12 and we hope or we think we will have a few more by the time we go live later this year. And in 2027, we will actually expand to more blockchains. So we start an Ethereum mainnet for obvious reasons, but we also see the need for us to be present in other chains as well. And this is where we are in a discussion with the community and with the builders to kind of figure out, so where else should we have a euro presence, you know, with deep liquidity and so on and so forth. So that's one of the points where we think there's going to be an interesting conversation between Kivalis and the DeFi community. It's also a conversation we're having with our banks because some of them are thinking in terms of maybe more permission chains or maybe more privacy oriented chains, which could be L2s, could be multiple ones. So a lot of different options out there. But there has to be a way for us to coordinate all of this, right? We cannot, you know, be everywhere at the same time. So that's going to be a process that probably will take a few years to, you know, identify all the chains. And our proposition or why we are reaching now to DeFi is, well, as I said, it's kind of the main bridge we see between TradFi and this new economy. So we're looking forward to talk with builders to integrating even more protocols. We're interested in decentralized exchanges, lending protocols, yield protocols.
