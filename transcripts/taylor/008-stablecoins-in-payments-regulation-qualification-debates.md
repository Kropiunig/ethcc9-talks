# Stablecoins in Payments: Regulation, Qualification Debates
**Julie Bader** (De Gaulle Fleurance) --- Taylor Stage

Is it working? Yes. Hi everyone. So it's my first time, so I'm going to try and understand how others work, but do not hesitate if you have any questions at the end of this presentation. So today we are talking about stablecoin and how they are reshaping the payment infrastructure. Just to present myself, I'm Julie Bader. I'm a fintech lawyer at De Gaulle Florence, and I work with financial institutions and fintech players in the crypto area. And for instance, we assisted Circle for them to obtain the license, the MICA license, to issue USDC and EURC in France and in the EU. So as a lawyer, we have the opportunity to see the project from the beginning, and we can see what is happening in the crypto and the payment area. And what is happening with stablecoin and payment is that it's a major shift that we see on payments. So as you may know, stablecoins have exploded from one year between 2024 and 2025. We have a 50% increase of stablecoin circulation. We have today around $270 billion stablecoins in circulation. So there is a big growth, but the growth of circulation is not the real story there. We see that the major shift is the change in the payment infrastructure. And so what we see is that companies that are using stablecoins for payroll, for payments, for cross-border treasury management, there is no longer crypto experimentation. It's using crypto stablecoins in the real business. What also we see is that stablecoins, they are not really replacing payments. They are reshaping the payments and rewriting the rails for payments. And this is not theory. Today we really see from our clients and the projects that we assist what is going on. For instance, you may have seen that Circle has partnered with Visa to allow USDC settlement transaction. We have also a lot of payment platforms that are using stablecoins for payouts and marketplaces that are using stablecoins for management of reimbursements. Why is there this usage of stablecoins? It's because it allows real-time settlement. They are available 24-7 and it implies lower costs. But the real innovation is not the token in itself, it's the infrastructure behind it. And we see that all the players, they are adapting to this new infrastructure and how stablecoin works. There is a lot of talk about banks being quite behind on this innovation, but what we see as lawyers is that the banks, they are moving towards stablecoins by becoming custodians for the reserve. We also see a lot of payment providers that are integrating stablecoins and crypto firms that are partnering with licensed institutions. So stablecoins is heating payments quietly and it's happening in a regulated environment, of course. And so, as you may know, in EU we have the MICA regulation that is applying to a stablecoin and entered into application mid-July 2024. And Circle was one of the first to obtain its license to issue a stablecoin in compliance with MICA. And what is happening with MICA is that it's created a regulated crypto asset that is stablecoin, based on euro, legal currency, but also it allows a defined framework for the stablecoin issuance in the EU. So MICA is a specific regulation, but as stablecoins are not only crypto, they also are e-money. They also are subject to the e-money directive that is another EU regulation. And as stablecoins are used as payment instruments, they also are subject to the PSD2, the Payment Services Directive. And so, what we face in France and in EU is that we have a complex web of regulation that is applying to stablecoins and has to be tackled on by actors and issuers and payment institutions so that they can use stablecoins in a regulated framework in the EU. Some say that the EU is blocking innovation by all this regulation, but what we can see is that even in the US, the regulation that is going to be applicable to stablecoins is really close to what we have in the EU in MICA. And so, it's our opinion as lawyers that as long as you are compliant with EU regulation, it will be easy to comply with the US regulation and all regulation around the globe that will be inspired by the MICA regulation. So, in practice, we see that it's very easy to already regulated institutions to go to stablecoins activities because they already are regulated and they are already ready on internal compliance or AMLCFT because they already have this infrastructure. But they lack the technological knowledge and infrastructure of crypto. And so, it's what we see, there is a convergence between the banks, the traditional financial institutions, and the crypto pure players that knows all about these things. What we say to our clients is that compliance is being a real advantage for all the players. For example, Circle is the company that chose to be regulated, the first regulated entity in the EU, and today they are the leaders on that. And they will be also the leaders in the US because they have chosen to go to compliance. So, what we say is that regulation is not slowing down innovation, it's structuring the market. And you have to understand this in order to succeed in the crypto and the stablecoins market. What we say is that the players who will succeed are the ones who understand both regulation and technology. And so, without them, you cannot scale compliantly and become the next leader in the stablecoins area. So, thank you. It was a short presentation, but if you have any questions, do not hesitate.
